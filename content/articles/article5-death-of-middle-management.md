# The Death of Middle Management: Everyone Becomes a TLM
# 中层管理的死亡：每个人都要成为 TLM

## The Translation Chain Is Breaking
## 翻译链正在断裂

**English:**

I watched it happen in a single meeting. An executive said: "We need to win enterprise." Three days later, a director presented a slide deck interpreting that sentence. A week after that, a manager had turned the slides into a roadmap. By the time an engineer got a ticket, the original intent was buried under four layers of formatting — and no one in the chain had added a single insight. They had only reformatted.

**Before:** A vague executive goal passed through meetings, docs, alignment calls, planning cycles, and reporting chains before a team could act. The middle manager's job was to carry the message and format the output.

**After:** An AI system can draft the strategy memo, convert it to a roadmap, break it into tickets, summarize progress, and generate weekly status updates — in an hour.

That kills the translator-only version of middle management. Not management itself. The translation layer.

The new bottleneck is not "can someone write the plan?" It is "can someone tell whether the plan is true?"

**中文：**

我在一次会议里亲眼看到这件事发生。高层说："我们要赢 enterprise。"三天后，director 做了一套 slide 来解释这句话。又过一周，manager 把 slide 变成了 roadmap。工程师拿到 ticket 时，原始意图已经被四层格式化埋掉——而翻译链上的每个人，都没有增加哪怕一个新的洞察。他们只是在换格式。

**之前：** 一个模糊的高层目标，要经过会议、文档、alignment call、planning cycle 和汇报链，团队才能开始行动。中层的工作是传递信息、格式化输出。

**之后：** AI 可以在一小时内起草 strategy memo、转成 roadmap、拆成 ticket、总结进度、生成 weekly update。

这会杀死只做翻译的中层管理——不是管理本身，是翻译层。

新的瓶颈不再是"有没有人能写计划"，而是"有没有人能判断这个计划是否真实"。

## Vision Reaches Execution Faster
## Vision 可以更快触达 Execution

**English:**

**Before:** Leader has an idea → director interprets it → manager breaks it down → PM writes a spec → engineers estimate → the plan slowly walks back up the chain for approval. Every layer adds context. Every layer adds delay.

**After:** A rough thought becomes a structured plan in minutes. The leader sees assumptions earlier. The executor sees strategic intent earlier. The team can challenge the plan before it calcifies into a quarter of wasted work.

This collapses the case for people whose main job is passing information between rooms. The slow information relay was why those roles existed. Remove the slowness and you remove the justification.

**中文：**

**之前：** Leader 有想法 → director 解释它 → manager 拆解 → PM 写 spec → 工程师估算 → 计划再慢慢往上走等 approval。每一层都增加 context，每一层也增加 delay。

**之后：** 一个粗糙想法，几分钟内就能变成结构化 plan。Leader 更早看到 assumptions，executor 更早看到 strategic intent，团队可以在计划变硬之前挑战它——而不是等整个季度白费后才发现问题。

这瓦解了"在房间之间传递信息"这类岗位存在的理由。信息流动慢，才是这些角色被需要的原因。去掉慢，就去掉了理由。

## Wider Scope Pulls Management Back Toward Building
## 更大的 Scope 把管理拉回 Build

**English:**

AI expands individual scope. An engineer can see more of product. A PM can understand more of implementation. A designer can prototype more of the workflow. A founder can inspect more of the execution plan.

The old IC-versus-EM split weakens. The person who only leads without building loses ground to the person who leads and builds.

The future looks more like TLM: technical lead manager. Someone who can translate, lead, and make.

A TLM turns vague intent into a feasible strategy, then stays close enough to execution to know whether the strategy survives contact with reality. They write part of the plan. They build part of the system. They review the tradeoffs. They notice when the AI-generated roadmap is elegant and impossible. They catch the gap between a clean strategy doc and the messy implementation path.

This is management with skin in the game.

**中文：**

AI 会扩大每个人的 scope。工程师能看见更多产品，PM 能理解更多实现，设计师能 prototype 更多工作流，创始人能检查更多执行细节。

旧的 IC 和 EM 分裂在弱化。只 lead 不 build 的人，会输给既 lead 又 build 的人。

未来更像 TLM：technical lead manager。一个能 translate、lead、make 的人。

TLM 把模糊 intent 变成可行 strategy，然后离 execution 足够近，知道 strategy 撞上现实后还能不能活。他写一部分 plan，建一部分 system，审 tradeoff，发现 AI 生成的 roadmap 哪些地方漂亮但不可行，抓住干净 strategy doc 和混乱 implementation path 之间的缝。

这是带 skin in the game 的管理。

## Big Companies Still Need Direction And Sponsorship
## 大公司仍然需要方向感和 Sponsorship

**English:**

At scale, vision gets political. Strategy crosses functions. Execution touches legal, finance, sales, security, and compliance. AI can surface the disagreement. It cannot resolve the politics.

An AI system only knows what you have managed to encode. It reads docs, tickets, CRM notes, Slack threads, dashboards. It does not know which customer pain is actually urgent this quarter, which internal metric is theater, or which executive sponsor can make a hard change real.

When AI generates ten plausible plans, someone still has to choose the direction. That someone has to find the leadership sponsor who can protect the work and make the organization care. Without direction, the team executes a beautiful plan toward the wrong hill. Without sponsorship, even the right plan dies in a priority meeting.

The weak translator says: "Here is what leadership wants." The strong one says: "Here is the feasible path. This part I will personally drive."

**中文：**

规模一大，vision 会变得政治化。Strategy 会跨 function，execution 会碰到 legal、finance、sales、security 和 compliance。AI 可以暴露分歧，但不能解决政治。

AI 只懂你已经成功编码进去的业务。它读文档、ticket、CRM note、Slack thread 和 dashboard——但它不知道哪个客户痛点现在最急，哪个内部指标只是表演，哪个 sponsor 能让困难变化真正发生。

AI 给出十个合理 plan 时，仍然需要有人选择方向。这个人还要找到 leadership 里的 sponsor，让这件事被保护，变成组织优先级。没有方向感，团队会把漂亮 plan 执行到错误山头。没有 sponsor，即使 plan 是对的也会死在优先级排队里。

弱 translator 说："这是 leadership 想要的。"强 translator 说："这是可行路径，这一部分我亲自推进。"

## The Objection: AI Will Learn The Organization Too
## 反对意见：AI 也会越来越懂组织

**English:**

The strongest objection: AI will get much better at company context. Connect it to Slack, Jira, Salesforce, GitHub, docs, meetings, and financial systems, and it will learn how decisions were made, which teams are overloaded, which projects keep slipping.

True. And exactly why weak translation dies faster.

If your value is remembering what happened in meetings, AI will remember more. If your value is forwarding status, AI will forward it faster. If your value is writing the first roadmap draft, AI will write it cheaper.

Defensible human value comes from judgment under incomplete context — even when every system is connected. Someone still has to decide which signal matters, which conflict deserves escalation, which customer pain should redirect the roadmap, and when a clean AI-generated plan is politically or operationally fake.

AI makes the map richer. Someone still has to choose the route.

**中文：**

最强的反对意见：AI 会越来越懂公司 context。接入 Slack、Jira、Salesforce、GitHub、文档、会议、财务系统，它就能学到决策过去怎么发生，哪些团队过载，哪些项目总是延期。

这是对的。所以弱翻译会死得更快。

如果你的价值是记得会议里发生过什么，AI 会记得更多。如果你的价值是转发状态，AI 会转得更快。如果你的价值是写第一版 roadmap，AI 会写得更便宜。

即使所有系统都接上了，人的可防守价值仍然来自在 context 不完整时做判断：哪个 signal 重要，哪个冲突值得 escalation，哪个客户痛点应该改变 roadmap，什么时候一个干净的 AI-generated plan 在政治上或操作上是假的。

AI 可以让地图更丰富，但仍然需要有人选择路线。

## What Dies And What Survives
## 什么会死，什么会留下

**English:**

**What dies:**
- Status relay.
- Meeting forwarding.
- Translation without judgment.
- Roadmap writing without feasibility ownership.
- People management disconnected from execution.

**What survives:**
- Judgment under ambiguity.
- Strategy under constraints.
- Cross-functional trust.
- Direction grounded in business reality.
- Sponsor-finding inside leadership.
- Execution ownership.
- The ability to turn fuzzy vision into a feasible path.

Middle management does not vanish in one clean cut. It compresses. The low-value layer disappears first. The high-value layer becomes more demanding.

The old middle manager translated between thinkers and doers. The new one has to think and do.

**中文：**

**会死掉的东西：**
- 状态转发。
- 会议转发。
- 没有判断力的翻译。
- 不拥有可行性的 roadmap 写作。
- 和执行脱节的 people management。

**会留下的东西：**
- 模糊环境下的判断。
- 约束条件下的 strategy。
- 跨 function 的信任。
- 扎在业务现实里的方向感。
- 在 leadership 里找到 sponsor 的能力。
- Execution ownership。
- 把模糊 vision 变成可行路径的能力。

中层管理不会被一刀切地消灭。它会被压缩。低价值层先消失，高价值层变得更难。

旧中层在 thinker 和 doer 之间做翻译。新中层必须同时 think 和 do。

## The Manager Becomes A Builder Again
## Manager 要重新成为 Builder

**English:**

AI makes information flow faster. Vision becomes strategy faster. Strategy becomes roadmap faster. Roadmap becomes execution faster.

The middle compresses.

The manager who survives cannot live on translation alone. They turn fuzzy intent into feasible strategy. They know which plan will break. They choose the right hill. They find the sponsor. They own a piece of the build.

The old question was: are you an IC or an EM? The new question is sharper: can you convert ambiguity into direction, sponsorship, and execution — and carry part of that execution yourself?

That is the death of middle management as we knew it. And the rise of the TLM.

Watch for this signal in your own organization: when AI starts generating "good enough" roadmaps and nobody challenges them, you are missing a TLM. The real risk is not that AI replaces managers — it is that managers let AI produce the plan and stop there, leaving direction and judgment on the table. Next: what the TLM role actually looks like inside a fast-moving AI company — the meetings they own, the calls they make, and where building fluency is still the job.

**中文：**

AI 让信息流动更快。Vision 更快变成 strategy，strategy 更快变成 roadmap，roadmap 更快变成 execution。

中间层被压缩。

能活下来的 manager，不能只靠翻译。他把模糊 intent 变成可行 strategy，知道哪个 plan 会碎，选对山头，找到 sponsor，并拥有一部分 build。

旧问题是：你是 IC 还是 EM？新问题更尖锐：你能不能把 ambiguity 变成方向、sponsorship 和 execution，并亲自扛起其中一部分？

这就是我们熟悉的中层管理之死。也是 TLM 的崛起。

在你自己的组织里留意这个信号：当 AI 开始生成"足够好"的 roadmap，而没有人挑战它时，你缺的就是一个 TLM。真正的风险不是 AI 取代管理者——而是管理者让 AI 生成计划后就停在那里，把方向感和判断力留在了桌上。下一篇：TLM 在一家快速发展的 AI 公司里具体是什么样的——他们拥有哪些会议，做哪些判断，以及哪些地方 building fluency 仍然是核心工作。
